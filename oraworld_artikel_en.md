## 1. About Me – My Journey

I am from Pakistan and have built my life in Frankfurt, Germany – from first grade all the way through to my Master's degree, everything here. Bachelor's in Business Informatics, Master's in Strategic Information Management, both at Frankfurt University of Applied Sciences. From the very beginning, IT and Business were never a contradiction for me – they were exactly the right combination. And right in the middle of that journey, Oracle APEX found me.

---

## 2. How Oracle APEX Shaped My Studies

My first real encounter with Oracle APEX was during my project semester – back then still with version 4.2. Six months, one semester, one application: a question-and-answer app for the classroom. The idea was simple but effective – the professor creates questions and answers in the app, students respond via their phones. Afterwards, the professor could immediately see which topics were well understood and where there were still gaps. No paperwork, no hassle – just direct feedback from the lecture hall.

That fascinated me: how quickly you can build a real, useful application with APEX. That feeling never left me – and so my Bachelor's thesis and later my Master's thesis were both implemented in Oracle APEX, both on topics that genuinely interested me and that I have never regretted.

---

## 3. From Theory to Practice – My Career

Straight after my Master's, I started as an Oracle APEX developer at Hyand – back then still known as MT AG. Seven years, continuously. That time changed my life.

What made those years so special was the environment. My manager was Niels de Bruijn – Oracle ACE Director and for me not just a boss, but a true mentor and role model. Through him and through constant exchange with other high-level Oracle ACE members, I was able to develop my knowledge year after year. I learned how to truly think as an APEX developer – not just how to do it.

On top of that, I had the chance to work directly on projects with colleagues like Moritz Klein – and through personal exchange with fellow colleagues like Philipp Hartenfeller or [Timo Herwix](https://www.linkedin.com/in/timoherwix/), I was constantly broadening my perspective. Every conversation, every shared experience shaped me as a developer and as a person. Hyand gave me not just technical knowledge – but an attitude, a standard of quality, and a network that stays with me to this day.

---

## 4. Founding S&H Software Solutions

After seven years at Hyand, it was time for my next step – my own. I had a vision that had been with me for a while: building Oracle APEX in Pakistan. I already knew friends there who worked with APEX, but the technology was barely known in the country. That's exactly where I saw the opportunity.

I started with friends – Awais Ahmed and Hassan Raza (now Oracle ACE). With a clear idea. We grew – not through rapid growth for its own sake, but step by step. First we brought in two, three more team members, then we rented our first office, then we became five, six, ten – and today we are a team of around 15 people.

What particularly excited me: I quickly realized that in Pakistan many developers use other technologies like Bubble or similar tools – Oracle APEX was barely on the radar. That was our chance, and we used it strategically.

Today we have over three senior developers in the team. That's no coincidence. It's the result of conviction, hard work and a clear goal: S&H Software Solutions should become the strongest Oracle APEX community company in Pakistan – and we build on that every single day.

---

## 5. The Idea Behind the Workshop

During my years at Hyand, I had seen how important structured learning is – how many people benefit when knowledge is shared clearly and consistently. That's exactly what I wanted to make possible for my team and the community here.

The result was a complete workshop based on Oracle APEX 26.1, consisting of 14 chapters – step by step, from zero to a finished application. All chapters are published on GitHub and can be worked through freely by anyone. That was important to me: no closed system, but something that belongs to the community.

The primary goal was to bring new and existing team members to a shared standard – consistent programming, consistent quality. All the knowledge I had gathered over the years, I wanted to pass on in a structured way. We ran the first session internally on June 1st, 2026 – with 14 participants.

But that was only the beginning.

---

## 6. The Workshop – Content & Structure

The workshop is divided into 14 chapters and guides you step by step through a complete Oracle APEX application – from zero to finished deployment. Each chapter includes real screenshots, clear instructions and no unnecessary theory. Just open it up and follow along.

01 – Set up a Workspace
02 – SQL Import
03 – Create an App
04 – Create a View
05 – Form (Low-Code)
06 – Create a Report
07 – Form with Package & API
08 – Smart Filter
09 – Plugin
10 – Login Design
11 – Functions & Procedures
12 – Automation
13 – Multilanguage (Urdu & English)
14 – Export & Deployment

What was important to me: the workshop is just as suitable for beginners as for developers who want to structure their knowledge. No PL/SQL experience required, just a browser. Each chapter takes around 20 minutes – manageable, practical, hands-on.

The entire workshop is available for free on GitHub: [github.com/Sajjad-786/oracle-apex-workshop](https://github.com/Sajjad-786/oracle-apex-workshop)

## 8. Looking Ahead – The Community is Growing

The internal workshop was the first step – but in my mind it was always clear that it wouldn't stop there. What we started offline in our office is, for me, just the proof of concept. The next step is to make the workshop available online – so that anyone, anywhere in the world, can join. No ticket, no registration – just open GitHub and get started.

What I care about most is the university plan. In Islamabad, where our office is, we already have contact with professors who teach database courses. The connection is personal – some of our own team members were once their students. That means something. When you work today with someone who was once your student, a circle closes. And that's exactly the circle we want to use to bring Oracle APEX directly into universities – for students who maybe don't yet know what's possible with APEX.

Pakistan has incredible talent. I see that every day in my team. What's often missing is not the ability – but the access, the structure, the awareness of what's out there. Oracle APEX is a technology that enables real applications in no time. And if we bring that into universities and online, we're not talking about a small project – we're talking about building an entire community.

## 9. Conclusion

When I look back today – from my first steps with Oracle APEX 4.2 in my project semester all the way to this workshop – I realize how much this journey means. Not because of the technology alone, but because of the people I met along the way, the knowledge I was able to build, and the opportunity to pass it on now.

I learned from others. From Niels, from colleagues, from every project. And now it's time to do the same – not because I have to, but because I know exactly what it means when someone opens a door for you. This workshop is my door for others.

One more thing I want to say: Oracle APEX is not a 9-to-5 job for me or my team. It has become a hobby. We stay later in the evening because a bug won't let us go, or because we just have to finish building a feature – not because we must, but because it drives us. And that's exactly what makes Oracle APEX so unique: you build something and immediately see live what came out of it. No long build process, no waiting – just build and see. That pulls you in.

With APEX 26.1, Low-Code and the integration of AI, entirely new paths are opening up right now. I believe the best is yet to come. Oracle APEX will become even more powerful, even more accessible, even more exciting in the years ahead. And those who are part of it now are in the right place at the right time.

My decision to choose Oracle APEX was the best decision of my professional life – and I don't regret a single second of it. I haven't just built something financially, but above all myself – my skills, my thinking, my own way. A strong team that shares the same passion. And by passing on knowledge, the community grows – piece by piece, person by person.

Oracle APEX deserves a strong community in Pakistan. We are working on it – and we are just getting started.
